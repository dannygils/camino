## Accommodation

Private rooms range widely depending on region and town size. The Basque Country
is the most expensive stretch — San Sebastián and Bilbao in particular. Costs drop
noticeably once you cross into Asturias and Galicia.

| Region | Stages | Nights | Est. per room/night | Est. per person |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basque Country | Days 1–7 | 7 | €70–100 | €35–50 |
| Cantabria | Days 8–14 | 7 | €55–80 | €28–40 |
| Asturias | Days 15–19 | 5 | €45–65 | €23–33 |
| Galicia | Days 20–31 | 13 | €40–60 | €20–30 |

> Prices are per room (double/twin), split between 2 people sharing.
> If anyone is in a single room, add ~€15–25/night.
> **Book well in advance** — June is peak season on the Norte, especially in
> San Sebastián, Bilbao, Santander, and Santillana del Mar.

### Nights with limited hotel options

A few stage endpoints have very little private accommodation — worth knowing early:

| Day | Town | Situation |
|---|---|---|
| Day 7 | Pobeña | Very small — limited options, consider staying in La Arena (1km prior) |
| Day 22 | Piñera | Tiny village — nearest hotels are in Luarca (16km back) or push on |
| Day 26 | Castromaior | Albergue only — no hotels. Consider stopping in Abadín (17km) instead |
| Day 28 | Sobrado dos Monxes | Limited — book the monastery albergue or Hotel San Marcus early |

## Food & Drink

Spain is good value for eating out, especially outside the Basque Country.

| Category | Budget option | Mid option | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Breakfast | €3–5 | €6–10 | Coffee + toast/pastry at a café |
| Lunch | €8–12 | €12–18 | Pilgrim menu (3 courses + wine) or bar bocadillo |
| Dinner | €12–18 | €18–30 | Pilgrim menu or restaurant à la carte |
| Snacks/coffee | €5–8 | — | Daily coffees, trail snacks, water |
| **Daily total** | **€28–43** | **€43–66** | Per person |

> The **pilgrim menu** (*menú del peregrino*) is the best value on the route —
> typically €10–14 for 3 courses + bread + wine/water. Available at most
> restaurants along the way for lunch and dinner.
>
> **Basque pintxos** in San Sebastián and Bilbao are a highlight but add up fast —
> budget an extra €20–30 for a proper pintxos evening in each city.

## Getting There & Home

| | Low | High | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flights to Bilbao or San Sebastián | €150 | €400 | Book early. Bilbao has better connections. |
| Train/bus Bilbao → Irún | €10 | €20 | ~1hr, straightforward |
| Train Santiago → airport | €5 | €15 | Santiago airport (SCQ) has good connections |
| Flights home from Santiago | €100 | €250 | Via Madrid or Lisbon if needed |
| **Total transport** | **€265** | **€685** | Per person |

> Flying into **Bilbao** and taking a bus/train to Irún (the start) is the
> easiest option. Flying out of **Santiago de Compostela** (SCQ) is convenient
> but has limited routes — Madrid or Lisbon connections are common.

## Tips for Keeping Costs Down

- **Book hotels 2–3 months ahead** for the Basque Country stages — last-minute
  prices in San Sebastián and Bilbao can be 2–3x higher in June
- **Pilgrim menus** at lunch are the best value meal of the day — most include
  wine, water, bread, and 3 courses for €10–14
- **Supermarkets** in larger towns (Bilbao, Santander, Gijón) are good for
  stocking trail snacks cheaply
- **Laundry**: washing by hand in the sink + a travel line saves €10–15/week
  per person vs machines
- **Guggenheim Bilbao** is worth the splurge (~€18) — budget an easy day in
  Bilbao for it
- **ATMs**: withdraw cash in larger towns. Some small stage-end towns (Pobeña,
  Piñera, Castromaior) have no ATM

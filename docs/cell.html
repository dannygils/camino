Overview

The Camino del Norte has generally better coverage than the Camino Francés because northern Spain is more densely populated and the route follows the coast and major towns. Most pilgrims report no significant dead zones. The main exceptions are:

  • Inland Galicia (Days 25–29) — the route turns away from the coast after Vilela and passes through very rural terrain. Mondoñedo, Castromaior, and Baamonde are the most likely weak spots.
  • Mountain sections mid-stage — signal can drop between towns on ascents, but recovers when you reach the next settlement.
  • Basque interior (Days 4–5) — Markina-Xemein and the Zenarruza monastery area are inland and hilly. Fine in towns, patchy on trail.

SIM Card Recommendations

Orange Spain 🥇

Best for pilgrims

Most recommended by pilgrims on the Norte. A store is right on the Camino in Irún as you leave town — easy to pick up on Day 1. Good rural coverage, cheap prepaid plans.

📻 Remote Work Planning (Joel)

Days to plan around

  • Day 26 – Castromaior & Day 28 – Sobrado dos Monxes: Plan these as offline days. Download everything the night before in Mondoñedo or Baamonde.
  • Days 24–25 (Vilela, Mondoñedo): Patchy. Schedule calls for midday when you can stop in a town centre café.
  • Best work days: Bilbao (Day 6), Gijón (Day 18), and Santiago (Day 31) all have 5G and are natural rest-day candidates.
  • Backup plan: Hotel WiFi is generally reliable throughout — the signal ratings above are for mobile data. Most hotels will have broadband.

Recommended setup

  • SIM: Movistar prepaid for best rural Galician coverage. Pick up in Bilbao if not Irún.
  • Offline tools: Download Maps.me or OsmAnd offline maps for Galicia before Day 24. Cache any work documents locally.
  • Hotspot: Consider a dedicated mobile hotspot device so others aren't draining your phone battery for connectivity.
  • Spain work visa: Ensure this is sorted before departure — see packing/packing-list.yaml.
